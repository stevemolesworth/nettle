Nettle Co.

British craft meets intimate ritual

Reality check

Making constraints

No access (yet) to:

  • Industrial leather machines
  • Advanced woodworking tools (lathe, etc.)

Limits: Perfect stitching, uniformity, luxury-level finish

Choice:

  • ✗ Compete with industrial perfection
  • ✓ Embrace handmade irregularity & character

Identity & mark

  • Lettermark: N
  • Slight "damage" / imperfection in the form — reflects pleasure/pain duality
  • Options: circle container, geometric form, botanical overlay (nettle leaf)

Typography

  • Cormorant Garamond — serif-led, British heritage feel
  • Tone: editorial, restrained, timeless
  • Use open-source equivalents initially

Materials palette

100% British / natural bias

Core materials:

  • Vegetable-tanned leather (UK)
  • Beech wood (handles)
  • Brass fittings
  • Waxed linen thread

Product system

Early thinking

Core components:

  • Handle
  • Strap / strop
  • Wrist restraints
  • Bit
  • Care oils / wax

Packaging concept

Premium, wooden case (beech and brass)

  • Padded presentation box
  • Harris Tweed / corduroy lining
  • Welsh lambswool

What does success look like?

Phase 1

  • Sell 1 → Proof of concept
  • Sell 2 → Break even
  • Sell 10 → Small profit

Goal: Validate demand + desirability. Have fun. Make a memory.

Pricing strategy

Target range: £500 → £2,000+

Justification:

  • Handmade
  • Limited runs (10–20 units)
  • Customisation
  • Emotional + experiential value

Margin: 100% (excluding time)
